Abstract

Every memecoin in existence chose its total supply arbitrarily. One billion. One trillion. Twenty-one million. These numbers were selected for aesthetic or technical reasons, not fundamental ones. $NUT takes a different approach.

The total supply of $NUT is derived from the estimated total number of human masturbation events documented across global research datasets spanning 2014 to 2026. This makes $NUT the first token whose monetary supply is grounded in an empirical behavioral dataset rather than a decision made in a Discord server.

The number is 2,869,000,000,000. This document explains how we arrived at it.

Section 1 — Why This Behavior as a Supply Basis

A sound monetary base requires the underlying asset to exhibit specific properties: scarcity relative to demand, measurability, consistency over time, and resistance to external manipulation. Gold exhibits these properties in the physical world. Bitcoin approximates them in the computational world. $NUT targets them in the behavioral world.

Human masturbation is among the most consistently documented behaviors in all of social science research. Unlike most human activities, it exhibits remarkably stable statistical properties across decades, cultures, and economic conditions. It does not correlate strongly with recessions, stock market performance, or political cycles. It has never gone to zero. It has never experienced a multi-year bear market.

The 2014-2026 period was chosen because it represents the full arc of widespread smartphone adoption through present day, creating for the first time a reasonably consistent global self-report methodology across multiple longitudinal studies. Prior to 2014, cross-cultural comparison data was insufficient for a defensible global estimate. The dataset is closed at the Genesis Snapshot date of May 27, 2026.

Section 4 — Derivation Formula

// Global adult population average 2014-2026
P_total = 5,200,000,000

// Participating adults (gender-weighted participation rates)
P_male = 5,200,000,000 × 0.504 × 0.738 = 1,931,270,400
P_female = 5,200,000,000 × 0.496 × 0.517 = 1,334,183,680
P_active = P_male + P_female = 3,265,454,080

// Weekly documented instances
W = (P_male × 2.14) + (P_female × 0.89)
W = 4,132,918,656 + 1,187,423,475
W = 5,320,342,131 per week

// Annual total
A = W × 52.18 = 277,618,248,000 per year

// Period total (2014-01-01 to 2026-05-27: 12.40 years)
D = A × 12.40 = 3,442,466,275,200

// Applied correction factor: 0.833
// Accounts for: age-band frequency decay, cross-cultural underreporting,
// variance in Western vs non-Western self-report fidelity,
// partial year weighting for 2025-2026 estimated from available data
SUPPLY = D × 0.833 = 2,867,574,207,000 ≈ 2,869,000,000,000

Section 8 — The Oracle Prediction Engine

The $NUT Oracle uses the behavioral profiles derived from this research to generate real-time predictions of global activity. These predictions are not financial advice. They are statistical estimates grounded in the same research datasets used to determine total supply.

The Oracle models four compound factors: hour-of-day profile (24 data points derived from platform behavioral timing data), day-of-week profile (NSSHB self-report data), month profile (Durex/NATSAL seasonal data with NNN anomaly encoding), and year-over-year trend index (smartphone adoption and COVID correction).

Personal predictions are generated from individual log history, blended 60/40 with the global profile to produce a combined next-window estimate. The Oracle tracks its own accuracy over time and displays it transparently on the leaderboard panel.

Section 9 — Immutability of Supply

The 2014-2026 snapshot period closed on May 27, 2026. The events it contains cannot be changed, added to, or removed. The supply of $NUT therefore cannot be inflated. There is no mechanism by which new tokens can be created to represent historical events that did not occur. This is not a policy decision. It is a property of time.

Future research covering periods after May 27, 2026 will not alter the supply. The dataset is closed. The number is fixed. The only asset whose supply is guaranteed not to increase because the past is not subject to revision.
